ARChef

Using augmented reality in recipe tutorials for amateur student cooks

what it is

ARChef (Augmented Reality Chef) is a mobile app that builds upon existing impersonal cooking applications by utilizing AR to simulate an in-person recipe tutorial for amateur college student cooks.

main features

  • 3D visualizations of tools and ingredients projected on user workspace, allowing users to mirror recipe instructions
  • Machine learning + image recognition through smartphone camera, alerting cook when they've completed a step or are in danger of making a mistake
  • Foundational tutorials, cooking dictionary, and financial tips to emphasize cooking education

what i did

role: user interface designer, user experience designer

skills: user interviews, affinity diagrams, wireframes, prototypes

context

5 month team project for HCI in Software Development course, design-based assignment with no technical constraints

process

research problem ideation design next steps

research

interviews with students

Based on 10+ interviews with individuals of various skill levels, we built a work activity affinity diagram from the group’s collective 100+ work activity notes.

takeaways:

  • Students believe that cooking is time-consuming and intimidating because of the initial learning curve
  • Students rely on friends and family for cooking aid
  • Students use online text recipes and YouTube recipes
  • Students want to cook to eat healthier, become more independent, and save more money
  • Dining halls and on-campus restaurants offer conveniency
interview with certified dietitian

We met with a registered dietitian who promotes balanced lifestyles through her cooking classes for college students. She offered us insight on student cooking and emphasized the importance of cost efficiency, personalization, and incremental learning.

problem


How might we create an app that encourages college students to be more healthy, independent, and financially savvy through home cooking?

ideation

From our research phase, we determined the following focuses:

  • Personalized learning experience with cooking
  • Heavily-guided recipes with visuals
  • Financial savings with home cooking

Inspired by Pokémon Go and Snapchat's uses of 3D visualizations through augmented reality, we wanted to incorporate this novel aspect into our own application to guide users in their kitchens and make the cooking experience more fun and personal.

After considering features such as AR, machine learning, and image recognition, we recognized the difficulty of switching between using a phone and cooking, we decided to implement voice commands and create a Still Mode and an Active AR Mode.

Still Mode: hands-off, projects the 3D visualizations onto a user-captured still image of the kitchen workspace so that students can set their phone down and follow along while cooking

Active AR Mode: hands-on use of phone for app’s machine learning and image recognition technology to determine if they’re ready for the next step in the recipe or if they're at risk of making a mistake (i.e. burning, overcooking)

design


Personalization: We included an onboarding questionnaire for users to customize their experience.

Financial benefits: To incentivize home cooking, the cost comparison of cooking at home versus eating out is shown on each recipe page.

Visual guidance: The AR tutorial, in combination with machine learning and image recognition technology, walks the user through each step of the recipe.

Cooking education: To further emphasize foundational skills, we included tutorials that students can utilize to reach their personal cooking goals.

next steps

Since this project was purely design-based and not technically-constrained, we explored options that may not be realistic in implementation. For this project to move further, we would have to put much more consideration into feasibility and do further research into technical limits. In terms of design, our next steps based on user testing that the rest of my team conducted with our prototype include:

  • Fleshing out transitions and button implementations in our mockup to minimize user errors during testing due to the mockup itself
  • Revisiting app navigation and information organization to improve learnability

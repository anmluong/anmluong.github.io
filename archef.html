ARChef

Using augmented reality in recipe tutorials for amateur student cooks

concept

ARChef (Augmented Reality Chef) builds upon existing impersonal cooking applications by utilizing AR to simulate an in-person recipe tutorial for amateur college student cooks, incorporating the novel AR aspect found in Pokémon Go and Snapchat.

main features

  • 3D visualizations of tools and ingredients projected on user workspace, allowing users to mirror recipe instructions
  • Machine learning + image recognition through smartphone camera, verbally alerting cook when they've completed a step or are in danger of making a mistake
  • Voice instructions and commands to facilitate use of app while cooking
  • Foundational tutorials, cooking dictionary, and financial tips to emphasize cooking education

context

role: UI/UX designer, prototyping lead

skills: user interviews, affinity diagramming, wireframing, prototyping

tools: Figma, Balsamiq

background: In Spring ‘19, I took HCI in Software Development, a project-based course in which teams design a solution to a problem of their choosing without consideration of technical constraints.

process

problem discovery design next steps

How might we create an app that encourages college students to be more healthy, independent, and financially savvy through home cooking?

discovery

We identified work roles to be recipe submitters (those who send in recipes to be transformed into AR tutorials) and recipe followers (those who learn from the AR tutorials) and interviewed 10+ individuals of various skills levels from these two roles. From the group’s collective 100+ work activity notes (extracted from raw interview notes), we built a work activity affinity diagram (WAAD).

trends in WAAD:

  • Students rely on friends and family for cooking aid → make cooking a more personal experience through AR
  • Students don’t know when something is done cooking → machine learning + image recognition
  • Students want to cook to save money and to become more independent & healthy

design

After comparing elements (e.g. onboarding, recipe overviews, etc.) of apps such Yummly, drawing out flow diagrams and conceptual designs, and storyboarding, we sketched ideas onto sticky notes, which I turned into wireframes on Balsamiq that were shown during a peer review session.

main takeaways from peer review:

  • Concerns about using phone while cooking → implement voice commands
  • Prioritize simplicity over visibility given tech-savvy college students → have 4 navigational tab options instead of 5
  • Prioritize educational aspect rather than social features → include cooking dictionary and foundational tutorials instead of an Instagram-like feed of posts

From this feedback, we created the mockup on Figma.

next steps

Based on the user testing that the rest of my team completed with the Figma mockup and physical prototype for the AR portion, our next steps include:

  • Fleshing out transitions and button implementations in Figma mockup to minimize errors during user testing due to the mockup itself
  • Revisiting app navigation and information organization to improve learnability
